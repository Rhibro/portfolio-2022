About

I have been moving around for the past couple years, I’ve gone from Canada, to Belgium, to Tanzania, and finally to Sweden. I feel I have now started a life in Malmo.

This summer (August 2022) I began my education at Sundsgården Folkhögskola studying Front-End Development. I am looking forward to starting on a new career path where I may draw on my previous knowledge from theatre and merge that with my new knowledge of front-end development.

I studied Theatre Performance at Humber College in Toronto, Canada. I’ve always loved studying and working in the arts. My education has helped me develop many skills such as working in big groups, taking on leadership roles, giving and receiving constructive criticism, and always keeping the audience in consideration.

How does the stage design/blocking/lighting/sound/music affect their experience? It is easy to get caught up in your own experience on stage and forget about the audience entirely. It’s important to have fun on stage, it’s also important to have a deep understanding of the play, but if you can’t communicate that to an audience then what’s the point?

My years in theatre have taught me how important it is to consider other people’s experiences. I’ve also learnt how to design purposefully. Everything on stage has a job no matter how small or unassuming i.e a vase or a carpet. Even the colours displayed have a purpose. Set design, lighting, and staging are all about drawing the eye to where you want the focus to be. I find some of the best set designs can be quite minimal with the focus being on the actors in space as opposed to crowded and cumbersome sets where the actors tend to get lost.

Within the theatre world you need to wear many hats. When you’re starting out you may need to be an actor, writer, producer, advertiser, designer, choreographer etc. I’ve been a part of productions where I have had all those titles. It takes a lot of logistical gymnastics to get a play from paper to stage.

Overall my experience in the arts has given me the skills to bring an idea into reality. I plan to bring that knowledge into my new career path.
